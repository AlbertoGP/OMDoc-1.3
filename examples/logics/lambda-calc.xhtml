Lambda Calculus
Michael Kohlhase
2002-04-16
The Theory of untyped Lambda Calculus.
Conceptlambda
This symbol is used to represent anonymous functions as lambda expansions. It is used in a binder that takes two further arguments, the first of which is a list of variables, and the second of which is an expression, and it forms the function which is the lambda extraction of the expression
Example

The following is a representation of the identity function: λXX

Conceptalpha-equal
The relation of alphabetic equality
Axiom

Bound Variables can be renamed, i.e. λXX=αλYY.

Conceptbeta-equal
The relation of beta equality
Axiom

For all lambda-terms A and B we have λXB(A)=β[A/X](B).

Example

λXX acts as the identity function: λXX(Y)=βY

Conceptbeta-reduces
The relation of beta reduction
Axiom

For all lambda-terms A and B we have λXB(A)→β[[A/X]/B].

Example

Beta equality need not be terminating in general: λXX(X)(λXX(X))→βλXX(X)(λXX(X))

Concepteta-equal
The relation of eta equality, this is a restricted form of extensionality
Axiom

For all lambda-terms B such that X is not free in B we have λXB(X)=βB.

Concepteta-reduces
The relation of eta reduction
Axiom

For all lambda-terms B such that X is not free in B we have λXB(X)→ηB.
