Substitution
Michael Kohlhase
2002-04-16
The Theory of Substitutions.
Conceptsubstitute
The elementary substitution constructor, it takes two arguments: the replacement term, the variable.
Conceptapply
The applicatio operator for substitutions, it takes two arguments: a substitution and a term.
Example

The expression [B/X] is the substitution operator that substitutes the term B for the variable X. It can be applied to a term by the substitution application operator. [B/X](A) this denotes the term that is obtained by replacing all free occurrences of the variable X with B in A.

Conceptsubstlist
The constructor for complex substitutions: it takes n arguments that are elementary substitutions.
